Issues in Tech

3.129.15

At Google, women hold onl 17% of the company's tech jobs. At Facebook, 15% of tech roles are staffed by women. At Twitter it's 10% (Huffington Post).

I think the problem is clear here. There has been lots of talk recently about how women are leaving the tech industry in droves (Los Angeles Times). At first I was suprised - there are now plenty of programs that encourage girls' interest in technology - but I shouldn't have been, of course, sexism and stereoyptes still prevail.

Whenever advice is given to women who work in a male-dominated industry is given, you are bound to hear something along the lines of "hard work excuses anything". Yes, of course, there is truth in that. But I think the problem is that once women do prove themselves, they are then asked again to prove themselves over and over again because they are women.

Unfortunately, stereotypes and misogynistic tendences are systematically embedded in society. They are created without us even knowing. Because of this I don't think there is an easy solution: it is not something that can be fixed over a short period of time.
